# Copy JTBD mapping

Before the interrogation runs, name who reads this copy and what they need to accomplish with it. Copy goals named without an audience serve the team's voice preferences; goals named for a ranked audience can be tested against a real reading need.

## What a copy JTBD is

A copy JTBD ("Jobs To Be Done") sentence names the progress a specific reader type hires this copy to make — not what the copy says, but what the reader uses it *for*. It is distinct from a generic persona description ("our user is a technical founder") because it names the situation, the action, and the goal at the moment the reader encounters this copy.

The template:

> **When** {situation}, **I want to** {action with this copy}, **so that** {goal I carry away}.

- *Situation* — the moment and context: evaluating a product in a trial, scanning a landing page after a referral, reading an announcement after an upgrade.
- *Action with this copy* — what the reader does with the words: quickly confirm the product is for them, understand what changed and why it matters, get a concise case to share with a decision-maker.
- *Goal I carry away* — the outcome the copy enables: enough confidence to start a trial, a clear reason to upgrade now, a quote or claim to use in an internal proposal.

One sentence per reader type. If the sentence needs a second clause to land, the job is not yet named — tighten it.

## The difference between a copy JTBD and an aesthetic JTBD

An aesthetic JTBD (from `creative-direction`) names what the reader *feels* — "When scanning this dashboard, I want to feel that this tool is reliable, so that I trust it before I act." A copy JTBD names what the reader *does* with the text — "When evaluating this pricing page, I want to confirm the plan covers my team's size, so that I can start the trial without asking sales." Both matter; they ground different kinds of goals. Copy direction uses the copy JTBD as its primary audience referent.

## Reader type ranking

After writing one sentence per reader type, rank them: primary, secondary, tertiary. The primary reader is the one whose job the copy must nail first. Copy that serves the primary reader precisely and does not reach the secondary reader is still a success; copy that tries to serve everyone equally serves none of them well.

Ranking criteria:
1. **Who is most likely to arrive?** The reader most likely to read this copy on this surface is the primary.
2. **Whose conversion matters most?** On a landing page, the reader closest to the conversion decision is primary. On an announcement, the reader most likely to share or act on the announcement is primary.
3. **Who would leave if you got it wrong?** The reader whose departure is most costly if the copy fails them is primary.

Record the ranked map before moving to Step 2. It becomes the Persona referent for every named copy goal.

## Exit

The map is ready when you can answer:

1. Who is the primary reader, and what job are they hiring this copy to do?
2. Are there secondary readers whose jobs conflict with the primary's? If so, name the trade-off.
3. If the copy serves the primary reader precisely, what does it sacrifice for each secondary reader — and is that the intended trade?
