# Copy JTBD mapping

Before the interrogation runs, name who reads this copy on this surface and what they need to accomplish with it. Copy goals named without a specific audience serve the team's voice preferences; goals named for a ranked surface audience can be tested against a real reading need.

## What a copy JTBD is

A copy JTBD ("Jobs To Be Done") sentence names the progress a specific reader type hires this copy to make on this surface — not what the copy says, but what the reader uses it *for*. It is distinct from a generic persona description because it names the situation, the action, and the goal at the moment the reader encounters this specific surface (a landing page, an above-fold hero, an announcement).

The template:

> **When** {situation}, **I want to** {action with this copy}, **so that** {goal I carry away}.

- *Situation* — the moment and context on this surface: evaluating a product during a trial, scanning a landing page after a referral, reading an announcement after an upgrade.
- *Action with this copy* — what the reader does with the words on this surface: quickly confirm the product is for them, understand what changed and why it matters, get a concise case to share with a decision-maker.
- *Goal I carry away* — the outcome the copy enables: enough confidence to start a trial, a clear reason to upgrade now, a quote or claim to use in an internal proposal.

One sentence per reader type. If the sentence needs a second clause to land, the job is not yet named — tighten it.

## Surface specificity

Copy-direction JTBDs are surface-specific, not brand-general. The same reader has different jobs on a landing page, a product announcement, and an onboarding surface — the copy direction for each surface must match the job on that surface, not an averaged job across all surfaces.

If the team cannot name the surface yet ("we just want to know how we sound"), this skill is not the right tool. Use `tone-of-voice` for general brand voice direction; use `copy-direction` when a specific surface is being built.

## Reader type ranking

After writing one sentence per reader type, rank them: primary, secondary, tertiary. The primary reader is the one whose job the copy must nail first on this surface.

Ranking criteria:
1. **Who is most likely to arrive?** The reader most likely to read this copy on this surface is the primary.
2. **Whose conversion matters most?** On a landing page, the reader closest to the conversion decision is primary. On an announcement, the reader most likely to share or act is primary.
3. **Who would leave if you got it wrong?** The reader whose departure is most costly if the copy fails them on this surface is primary.

Record the ranked map before moving to Step 2. It becomes the Persona referent for every named copy goal.

## Exit

The map is ready when you can answer:

1. Who is the primary reader on this surface, and what job are they hiring this copy to do?
2. Are there secondary readers whose jobs conflict with the primary's? If so, name the trade-off.
3. If the copy serves the primary reader precisely, what does it sacrifice for each secondary reader — and is that the intended trade?
