Product Strategy is the seat upstream of product engineering and experience design. Three pillars: **market & competitive strategy** (SWOT, Porter's Five Forces, PESTLE, BCG Matrix, OKR cascade, PRFAQ, stakeholder-research synthesis) that produce committed artifacts in `docs/product/shaping/` and feed the PE pack's shaping queue; **UX strategy** (experience vision, goals/measures with value loop and adoption hypothesis) upstream of journey-mapping; and **content strategy** — the Halvorson quad (Purpose · Process · Structure · Governance) above per-surface content design.

The pack now explicitly owns the **adoption hypothesis**, **first-success event**, **value loop**, and **metric tree** — the four fields that connect strategy to outcome. A strategy that ships without naming what behavior constitutes adoption, or how user behavior causes the north-star metric to move, is a vision statement, not a strategy. Nine skills produce the 14-point strategy output that feeds the Digital Experience Contract's Strategy section: situation analysis through differentiation, adoption hypothesis, Metric Tree, and strategy-to-experience handoff.
